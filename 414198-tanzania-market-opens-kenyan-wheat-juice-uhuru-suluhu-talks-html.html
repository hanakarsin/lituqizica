    Tanzania Market Opens up for Kenyan Wheat, Juice after Uhuru, Suluhu Talks

    - Trade ministers from both countries deliberated on 64 issues, out of which 30 were resolved - Tanzania will allow Kenyan wheat and juice, while Kenya agreed to return the favour by granting similar treatment to Tanzanian cement

    - Tanzania will allow Kenyan wheat and juice, while Kenya agreed to return the favour by granting similar treatment to Tanzanian cement

    - The revenue authorities will explore the possibilities of allowing free movement of cigarettes made from raw materials sourced from Tanzania

    Tanzania has agreed to grant wheat flour and soft drinks from Kenya preferential treatment and free access to its market.

    The declaration followed a bilateral meeting between Trade Cabinet Secretary Betty Maina and her Tanzanian counterpart, Kitila Mkumbo, to remove trade cross-border barriers.

    Tanzanian paper, The Citizen, on Wednesday, May 2, reported that the neighbouring countries deliberated on 64 issues, out of which 30 were resolved.

    Further engagements would be convened later, during which key decisions would be made on how to settle the remaining 34.

    President Samia Suluhu-administration allowed in Tanzania Kenya's wheat flour and juice.

    On its part, Kenya agreed to return the favour by granting similar treatment to Tanzanian cement in its market.

    “Some of the issues resolved on the side of the United Republic of Tanzania include facilitating clearance of soft drinks such as juices, removal of inspection fees for processed products with standardisation mark including wheat flour,” read the communique.

    On its part, Kenya agreed to return the favour by granting similar treatment to Tanzanian cement in its market.

    With both countries looking to fully implement a single customs territory, the revenue authorities will explore the possibilities of allowing free movement of cigarettes made from raw materials sourced from Tanzania.

    Suluhu-Uhuru talks

    During her maiden visit to Kenya on Wednesday, May 5, Suluhu and her host, President Uhuru Kenyatta, agreed to eliminate barriers to trade and the movement of people.

    Uhuru asked the relevant government authorities to ensure Tanzanian maize held at the border was released in two weeks.

    At the same time, the head of state allowed Tanzanian traders to operate in Kenya without visas and work permits.

    "Our investors from Tanzania should now know that they have the freedom to bring their products and do their businesses in Kenya without their business visas and work permits but they should follow the laid out laws and protocols," he said.

    Uhuru also called on Kenyans and Tanzanians to stop competing against each other to attract investments, create jobs and grow both economies.
