    Nairobi Governor Mike Sonko's leaked phone conversation with Kiambu Governor Ferdinand Waititu on Wednesday, August 15, left many questions than answers on many Kenyans' minds.

    Having desperately reached out to his long time friend to save his wife Susan Wangare following her arrest, Waititu's call to Sonko painted a clear image of a society described in George Orwell's allegorical novel, The Animal Farm.

    If I may call this a blessing in disguise, Kenyans can now understand the injustices that go on behind closed doors in state offices, and why seeking justice is an uphill task for the ordinary man.

    Sonko was heard saying he will order for the release of his Kiambu counterpart's wife, an indication that the Nairobi governor was willing to subvert justice for his 'friend's sake.

    This is despite Wangare having been arrested for allegedly putting up a high-rise building without a permit.

    TUKO.co.ke outlined five lessons Kenyans learned from the disturbing phone conversation which has now put the Nairobi governor on the spot.

    1. Sonko is part of the problem

    Sonko is part of the problem ailing the state: corruption. He openly defended and protected an illegality even as the government tried so hard to fight impunity which has seen many illegal building put up.

    READ ALSO: Waititu used to copy my answers during exams in campus - Sonko

    2. State House controls Nairobi county

    Sonko openly told Waititu that Uhuru himself is controlling the demolition of illegal building in the country.

    He even referred Waititu to the president if he needed any more help regarding the freedom of other 14 people arrested together with his wife.

    3. Kenya is a bandit economy

    While it might be obvious Kenya is a bandit country, the conversation depicted a bandit economy in which the rich or who is who in the society get away with anything for as long they are connected.

    4. Sonko is equally corrupt

    It has also been a bitter lesson that Sonko is just as corrupt as those he appears to be fighting. Asking Waititu to come and get a building approval for a building that has already been put up does not make him any different from other corrupt Kenyans who go for short cuts.

    READ ALSO: Aspiring MP among cons posing as DP Ruto's security arrested with over 700 laptops

    5. Mike Sonko is biased

    He is not only corrupt buy biased in that he attempted to have Waititu's wife protected while the rest of the suspects arrested alongside her were to face the law simply because he could not save all of them.

    He was heard ordering for the release of Waititu's wife on a cash bail while all others arrested alongside her be left to face the law.

    Well the Wednesday, August 15, phone call will go down memory lane as one that clearly defined how the law favours a selected few in the society

    It will also be remembered as a conversation that described Sonko as an obstruction to the same law he claims to be protecting.
