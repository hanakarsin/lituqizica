    Governor Kivutha Kibwana signs KSh 2.5 billion deal with WFP to promote agriculture in Makueni

    - The county government has entree into a five-year programme to boost agriculture in the county - It will be funded on a 50-50 basis for multi-sectoral developments - By 2023 at least 44,000 residents are expected to benefit directly and over 200,000 indirectly

    26 Jul 2024, 00:00

    - The county government has entree into a five-year programme to boost agriculture in the county

    - It will be funded on a 50-50 basis for multi-sectoral developments

    - By 2023 at least 44,000 residents are expected to benefit directly and over 200,000 indirectly

    Makueni Governor Kivutha Kibwana has signed a KSh 2.5 billion deal with the World Food Programme (WFP) to support sustainable food systems in the county.

    The five-year program which based on a 50-50 funding for multi-sectoral value chains development, was signed by Kibwana and WFP deputy country director Lara Fossi in Nairobi on Friday, June 28.

    In a Facebook post by the county boss, the program targets smallholder producers, youth, market and supply chain actors, food-insecure and vulnerable populations to benefit from sustainable, resilient inclusive systems and meeting food and nutrition needs by 2023.

    The deal seeks to promote scaling up of climate resilient technologies and best practices for improved and sustainable smallholder food production systems and enhance sustainable range land management for improved livestock production and enhanced community resilience.

    Further it will improve market and supply chain linkages, including reduction of post-harvest losses and opportunities for value addition and strengthen technical and institutional capacity to deliver sustainable hunger solutions.

    By the end of five years, the county is expected to have rehabilitated over 10,000 acres of degraded range land and increased access to safe and reliable water for both domestic, livestock and irrigation use.

    At least 44,000 residents are expected to benefit directly and over 200,000 indirectly.

    However, it was not the first for the governor who has been identified among Kenyans as the most hardworking to make such a bold move for the benefit of his residents.

    Among other important projects that he has launched in his county include a milk processing plant and the Makueni mother and child hospital which was launched by First Lady Margret Kenyatta.
