    Tension has gripped the West African nation of Mali after a group of military soldiers reportedly arrested and detained interim president, Bah Ndaw, and prime minister, Moctar Quane, following a controversial Cabinet reshuffle.

    The military on Tuesday, August 19, stormed Bamako and arrested President Keita alongside senior state officers.

    The BBC reported that the country's defence minister, Souleymane Doucoure, was also arrested alongside the two.

    "The soldiers drove the trio to the notorious Kati military base approximately nine miles outside the country's capital."

    TUKO.co.ke understands that the Monday, May 24, detention of the three senior state officials followed a controversial Cabinet reshuffle that led to the removal of two junta members who were part of the military soldiers that overthrew Mali’s government in 2020.

    "As a group, we demand the immediate and unconditional release of these authorities.
