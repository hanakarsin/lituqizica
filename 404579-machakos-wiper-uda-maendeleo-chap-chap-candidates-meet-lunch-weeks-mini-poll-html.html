    Three contenders for Machakos senatorial mini poll have met over lunch a few weeks to the election day.

    Wiper Democratic Movement (WDM) candidate Agnes Kavindu, United Democratic Alliance (UDA)'s Urbanus Ngengele and Maendeleo Chap Chap's Simeon Kitheka shared a table at a Machakos hotel on Tuesday, March 2.

    Read also

    Jovial and lively

    The three candidates were accompanied by a few of their supporters and were captured exchanging pleasantries before leaving for separate campaigns.

    In a video clip seen by TUKO.co.ke, Kavindu, Kengele and Kitheka were visibly happy and engaged each other in their local dialect as their supporters interacted separately in groups.

    The Machakos senatorial by-election is slated for March 18, and campaigns are in top gears.

    The seat was left vacant following senator Boniface Mutinda Kabaka's demise, who was elected on the Wiper party ticket.

    The Kalonzo Musyoka-led outfit has been working overtime to recapture the seat, which has attracted a lot of political interests.

    High stakes

    Deputy President William Ruto, who is likely to meet with Kalonzo in the presidential race in 2022, is determined to wrestle the seat from Wiper's fold through the UDA party.

    Machakos Governor Alfred Mutua, who doubles up as the Maendeleo Chap Chap party leader, has also declared his presidential ambitions and is keen on showcase his political tentacles in the county by winning the senatorial seat.

    In a bid to fend off the forays and inroads being made by Wiper opponents, Kalonzo has sought the back up of his political buddies, among them Musalia Mudavadi (ANC), Moses Wetang'ula (FORD Kenya) and Gideon Moi (KANU).

    In mid-February, the four politicians camped in Machakos and conducted a series of joint rallies to drum up support for Kavindu.

    They have also carried similar campaigns in Matungu and Kabuchai constituencies where ANC and FORD Kenya are fighting to recapture their seats following the demise of respective MPs.
