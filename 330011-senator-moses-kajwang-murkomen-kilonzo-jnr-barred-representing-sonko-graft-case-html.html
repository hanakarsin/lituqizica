    Senator Moses Kajwang wants Murkomen, Kilonzo Jnr barred from representing Sonko in graft case

    - Senator Moses Kajwang wants law formed to prevent state officers from doing business in circumstances that result in conflict of interest - He has bashed lawyers defending such practice by citing Senator Orengo as a pacesetter stating that two wrongs don't make a right

    01 Jan 0001, 00:00

    - Senator Moses Kajwang wants law formed to prevent state officers from doing business in circumstances that result in conflict of interest

    - He has bashed lawyers defending such practice by citing Senator Orengo as a pacesetter stating that two wrongs don't make a right

    - The senator argues that the work of senators is to defend devolution and so those representing people accused of graft are killing counties

    - Nyatike MP Tom Odege believes politicians representing people accused of corruption are seeking political millage

    - President Uhuru earlier bashed Murkomen and Kilonzo stating the Attorney General will draft policies that will put such practise in check

    - The duo is among lawyers in Sonko's defence team in a case where the governor has been charged with pilferage of KSh 357 million

    The Senate Public Accounts Committee (PAC) Chairman Moses Kajwang has lashed out at Senators Kipchumba Murkomen (Elgeiyo Marakwet) and Mutula Kilonzo Junior (Makueni) for representing Nairobi Governor Mike Sonko in a graft case he is facing.

    Speaking on Saturday, December 14, Kajwang who is Homa Bay Senator, criticised his colleagues stating that their actions were aimed at watering down efforts by the Senate to act as watchdogs for county operations.

    Kajwang further took issue with senators defending themselves from such practice by claiming the likes of James Orengo (Siaya) were doing it stating that two wrongs had no capacity to make a right.

    "It is not right for my colleagues to do it. I have had some argument that Senator Orengo and Senator Okong'o Omogeni (Nyamira) did it in the past.One wrong does not make another right...we must all come out and say that we draw clear lines. When you make a decision and ask the people to elect you to the Senate, you know what your specific mandate is going to be," said Kajwang.

    The lawmaker's sentiments come two days after President Uhuru Kenyatta indirectly blasted Murkomen and Kilonzo describing their appearance in court to defend Sonko as a pure conflict of interest.

    Speaking during commemoration of Jamhuri Day at Nyayo Stadium on Thursday, December 12, Uhuru said the Attorney General was going to draft guidelines that would check such a kind of practice.

    "A teacher cannot have one foot in the classroom and another one in parliament, you cannot serve as a legislator in national or county assembly and serve in the legal profession.Similarly, judges and magistrates should cease completely any practice of law while in office, doctors, engineers and other professionals who take up state or public jobs should give up private practice," said Uhuru.

    The PAC boss has, therefore, proposed formulation of laws that will prevent public servants from doing business in circumstances that result in conflict of interest.

    "We need to put in legislation that will bar public and state officers from doing business in situations that will constitute a clear conflict of interest," said Kajwang.

    The Senator's remarks were echoed by Nyatike MP Tom Odege, who described the appearance of Murkomen and Kilonzo in court as a means of seeking political mileage at the expense of the citizens.

    He urged lawmakers representing Sonko to let the embattled governor carry his own cross and use other advocates in proving his innocence.

    "When you look at the charges being preferred on Sonko....I will say that it is important that we let him carry his own york.Let him stand in court and answer the questions by himself. Politicians should not use corruption cases to seek political mileage," said Odege.
