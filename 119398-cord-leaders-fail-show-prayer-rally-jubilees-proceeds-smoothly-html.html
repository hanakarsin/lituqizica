    CORD leaders busy with private agendas, no time for rally

    - CORD leaders Raila, Wetangula and Kalonzo failed to show up at their prayer rally that was said to stand with the post elevtion violence victims - Each had a personal agenda to carry out as members of the coalition took over the event in their absence

    04 May 2024, 00:00

    - CORD leaders Raila, Wetangula and Kalonzo failed to show up at their prayer rally that was said to stand with the post elevtion violence victims

    - Each had a personal agenda to carry out as members of the coalition took over the event in their absence

    CORD leaders Raila Odinga, Moses Wetangula and Kalonzo Musyoka were not in attendance of their rival prayer rally thatwas touted as an event to stand with victims of the post election violence of 2007/2008 considering they’d earlier sworn to attend.

    The three missed out on the event held in Kibra slums in Nairobi which was also meant to be a move to oppose Jubilee’s thanksgiving ceremony that was held at Afraha stadium in Nakuru county at the same time.

    According to Citizen TV, Raila was still on his tour of France as Kalonzo travelled to Portugal and Wetangula is on a mission to strengthen Ford Kenya party in Trans Nzoia and Bungoma counties.

    Narc Kenya leader Martha Karua who had also said she would attend the event cancelled at the last minute but promised to attend any future events that the coalition held.

    READ ALSO: The big question Kenyans are asking as Uhuru, Ruto head to Nakuru for prayers

    The opposition event was touted as one that would show that the wrangles embroiling the coalition would not split the three parties but the absence of the three leaders left more questions than gave answers to the public who followed their moves ahead of the 2017 general election.

    By noon time today Saturday, April 16, the public had already begun gathering at  the Kibra grounds as other CORD politicians arrived at the event an hour later.

    Social media was awash with hostile remarks as attendants of both rallies and those following the events from home threw jibes at each other as to how the international criminal court cases were handled.

    READ ALSO: Martha Karua reacts to Raila's controversial remark on termination of ICC cases

    The Jubilee prayer rally was announced two weeks ago after the ICC dropped charges against Deputy President William Ruto and radio journalist Joshua Sang.

    The choice of their location was also significant as this was the exact same place Uhuru and Ruto launched the Jubilee alliance that went on to win the 2013 general election.

    They are expected to announce their campaign bids to remain incumbents in next years election at this event.
