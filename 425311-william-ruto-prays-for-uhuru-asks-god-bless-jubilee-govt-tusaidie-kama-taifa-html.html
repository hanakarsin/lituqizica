    A video of Deputy President William Ruto praying for his boss President Uhuru Kenyatta, despite the two leaders being at loggerheads, has gone viral.

    The second in command conducted the prayers at his official Karen residence on Tuesday, September 7, when he held a consultative meeting with grassroots leaders and opinion shapers from Gatanga, Murang'a county.

    Ruto flanked by Kang'ata during the prayers

    During the prayer session, Ruto was flanked by Murang'a senator Irungu Kangata and lawyer Edward Muriu.

    ''We present the president before you and all those of us serving under him and the entire leadership of this country so that God can help us achieve and know your love for our beloved country,'' Ruto said.

    The DP also prayed to God to intervene and help resolve the country's challenges, including the COVID-19 pandemic.

    ''We also pray over the challenges we are facing as a country such as the COVID-19 pandemic that you may give us skills and knowledge on how best we can tame it to open our economy and continue worshipping you in churches.'

    Ruto prayed for a united Kenya

    He also prayed for unity among Kenyans appealing to God to help end tribalism and poverty in the country.

    ''We also pray that you may help us achieve all our goals and provide food for those who lack it,'' said Ruto, who equally prayed for all political aspirants eyeing various seats in the 2022 General Election that they achieve their dreams.

    Uhuru and Ruto fell out after the March 9, 2018, handshake between the head of state and his once-erstwhile rival Raila Odinga.

    The move has been attributed to the lethal injection that killed the bromance between Uhuru and Ruto displayed ahead of the 2017 polls.

    Tuju slams Ruto

    In what signalled the end of the Jubilee Party, secretary-general Raphael Tuju on Monday, September 6, slammed the deputy party leader and deputy president, accusing him of, among other things frustrating Uhuru.

    Tuju, in a hard-hitting statement, questioned the source of the DP' wealth, demanding that he discloses how much he has paid the Kenya Revenue Authority (KRA) from his KSh 1.5 million a day egg sale.

    In the sarcastic statement, Tuju urged the DP to do the honourable thing and leave the party to focus on his newly formed United Democratic Movement (UDA) party.
