    Nominated senator Isaac Mwaura has said Deputy President William Ruto and ODM leader Raila Odinga are the favourites in the 2022 General Election.

    The two horses

    According to the senator, the 2022 presidential battle will be between Raila and Ruto; the rest of the candidates still have much work.

    "They are the two Rs who are the kingpins and we must agree that the other ones will have to do a lot of work," he said.

    At the same time, he said the Mt. Kenya region might not vote as a block since the area is already divided by the two candidates.

    "Where are we going to see the final decision by the majority of the people, where are they going to be? You cannot say the region will have 100% because even when the region has its candidate, the turn out has never been 100% vote," Mwaura said.

    However, the lawmaker noted that the majority of the people in Mt. Kenya are on the side of Ruto, saying that to sell Raila in the region will require a lot of work to be undone.

    He held that even the Jubilee leader are avoiding Raila's tour internationally because they don't want to be associated with him.

    "Even the allies of the president, very few of them have been seen going with Raila to the ground. In my own opinion and other opinion poll that have been done, Ruto is more popular than Raila,"

    Raila going for Mt. Kenya votes

    In a separate story by TUKO.co. ke, Raila said he plans to take over the vote-rich Mt Kenya region ahead of the 2022 General Election.

    Speaking at the funeral of Mzee Ajwang Nyakwamba on Friday, August 13, in Homa Bay county, the former prime minister said he would hunt for votes in the Central region as he seeks his fifth stab at the presidency.

    The perennial presidential candidate who has lost in all four elections he has participated in expressed confidence he would win the hearts of Mt Kenya voters.

    "I have seen ahead. I have seen where we will pass...Others said we will not climb the mountain but I have seen the right way," Raila said in his Dholuo language.
