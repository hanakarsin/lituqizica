    An Irish national has lost a multi-million house, 10 plots worth millions to his estranged Ugandan lover with whom he was romantically involved since 2002.

    David Michael O’Connel and the mother of his three children have been in a protracted court battle over the ownership of the assets they acquired during their union.

    According to Daily Monitor, the court ruled that since the properties were in the Ugandan woman's names, it would not go into the nitty gritties of whether the Irish national was the financier.

    Pseudo marriage

    In her petition, the mother of three was seeking to have her lover denied access to the properties, annulment of their union and full custody of their children on grounds that he was cruel to them.

    However, the court found that there was no marriage to terminate as the two had were never married in the first place.

    Nakawooza was, nonetheless, given full custody of the children on grounds that they are young and they need their mother's care.

    The Irish national who expressed his dissatisfaction with the court's decision noted he would appeal the ruling in 14 days.

    Court annuls marriage

    In a separate report, TUKO.co.ke reported when a magistrate's court in Nakuru annulled a five-year marriage between a man and his wife who is a lesbian.

    The church elder who told the court that his marriage lacked intimacy filed for divorce a few weeks ago citing his wife's sexual orientation which complicated the union.

    In the court ruling, Nakuru Principal Magistrate Yvonne Khatambi concluded the marriage was irretrievably broken and thus annulled it.
