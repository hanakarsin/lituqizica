    8 typical new year resolutions every Kenyan is likely to make and break

    It is common for everyone to have resolutions and goals they wish to achieve each year. As the world marks the end of 2019 and conclusion of the first decade of the new millennium, Kenyans are never left behind in making new year resolutions.

    09 Mar 2024, 00:00

    It is common for everyone to have resolutions and goals they wish to achieve each year.

    As the world marks the end of 2019 and conclusion of the first decade of the new millennium, Kenyans are never left behind in making new year resolutions.

    There are eight resolutions likely to appear in most Kenyans' list as compiled by TUKO.co.ke and they are quiet hilarious.

    1. Lose Weight

    Majority might have gained weight and feel that they need to watch themselves. Since 2019 is over, expect this to be in almost each Kenyan 2020 resolutions.

    Actually, the majority of them are already in groups that offer support and help in weight loss.

    The only problem is its never a walk in the park as many drop along the way as soon as they begin.

    2. Start going to the gym

    Most will think that this is similar to the one above but the fact is most people go to the gym to tone their bodies and keep fit.

    There are many other activities that take place in the gym as some go to flex while men go to look for perfect abs and women want to attain that figure.

    You can be sure that most Kenyans have already started passing by their hood gyms to inquire on the charges.

    Again, not an easy resolution to keep for obvious reasons.

    3. Eat right

    This I'm sure has never missed in majority of Kenyans' new year resolutions.

    The question is how many follow it and for how long do they maintain a healthy diet?

    Eating right is mostly to help one watch their health and maintain a good body.

    4. Start Saving

    Without even saying much, this is already in the minds of all Kenyans considering that 2019 has not been an easy one for many.

    Financially, people have been depressed and many feel that the only way to remain sane is keeping a small share of their earnings either in a bank account, Sacco or a home bank.

    According to Kenyans, most will say that this will help them cope with the different things that might strike in the year.

    But this one is easier said than done, trust me!

    5. Cut off fake friends

    I do not know if this works but going by majority WhatsApp, Facebook and Instagram statuses, it is already a set resolution.

    Kenyans have over and over again maintained that one does not need to stick around friends who do not help or contribute in one's life.

    Others feel that they need to lay off backbiters and for fear of being back stabbed.

    Do not go far to confirm this, just go through your friends' accounts and you will be surprised to find this at the top of their 2020 resolutions.

    You may even be shocked to find you have even been chopped off the friends' list.

    6. Get married

    This will be at the top of the list for most Kenyans especially those who are hitting the ages of 25-30.

    Honestly, I do not know what happens with this considering that at the end of each year, most say it was difficult to achieve it.

    You can be sure that this will not miss in the resolution list but the question is, are there suitable suitors in this era of 'eating fare'?

    7. Buy a car

    Every Kenyan who is working and feels the traffic heat within the city is already drafting this.

    Locals are known to value cars and for the longest time, a car has been used to measure one's social status.

    But cars are not bicycles and require one to dig deeper in their pockets, otherwise, for those desiring vehicles, things on the ground are very very different.

    8. Get saved and build a better relationship with God

    Being the last day of the month, many are already preparing themselves to end the year 2019 in style in various joints in the country.

    This is a resolution that comes up on the first of every January each year while most realise they spent too much on the new year eve.

    As they say, Kenyans will always be Kenyans in that messing up is something that gives them a call to action to go closer to their God.

    This time you will see many being online preachers as well as motivational speakers for a few weeks before they resume to their old habits.
