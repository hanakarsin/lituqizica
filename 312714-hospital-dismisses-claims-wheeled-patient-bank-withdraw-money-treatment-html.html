    Hospital dismisses claims it wheeled patient to bank to withdraw money for treatment

    - Photos of a man on a wheel chair in a banking hall went viral on social media - It was alleged the man had gone to the bank to get money before being attended to by Nakasero Hospital - The facility dismissed the claims saying it had not received such a patient

    15 Jun 2024, 00:00

    - Photos of a man on a wheel chair in a banking hall went viral on social media

    - It was alleged the man had gone to the bank to get money before being attended to by Nakasero Hospital

    - The facility dismissed the claims saying it had not received such a patient

    A hospital in Kampala, Uganda, has come out to defend itself against allegations that it wheeled a patient to a bank to withdraw money before commencing treatment.

    Nakasero Hospital was forced to respond after photos of a patient in a wheel chair at a banking hall circulated online indicating the facility had demanded for payment before attending to him.

    Following public outcry after the photos went viral on social media, the hospital came out and dismissed the claims saying it had not received such a patient at the facility.

    "We have noted a malicious post on one of the social media platforms purporting that Nakasero Hospital transported a patient to a bank to access funds prior to medical treatment. We would like to categorically state that this is not true; this patient was not from our facility," "As a leading healthcare provider in the region, Nakasero Hospital always puts patient care above all else. We expect the person responsible for this post to withdraw it immediately," said the hospital in a statement.

    In the post, a visibly ill elderly man is seen seated on a chair with other people standing by his side, presumably a healthcare provider and his family members.

    On the other side of the table is another man busy on a computer presumably attending to the man on the wheelchair.

    Further review of other items in the photos indicated the man was in an Equity Bank or in a building that is next to the banking hall.

    The bank is reported have given him the money to attend to his medical condition.
