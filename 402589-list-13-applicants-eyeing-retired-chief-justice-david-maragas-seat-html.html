    List of 13 applicants eyeing retired chief justice David Maraga's seat

    - The applicants are seeking to succeed David Maraga who retired on Tuesday, January 12, upon the attainment of 70 years - None of the Supreme Court judges, including Deputy Chief Justice Philomena Mwilu and Njoki Nding'u applied for the top job

    29 Mar 2024, 00:00

    - The applicants are seeking to succeed David Maraga who retired on Tuesday, January 12, upon the attainment of 70 years

    - None of the Supreme Court judges, including Deputy Chief Justice Philomena Mwilu and Njoki Nding'u applied for the top job

    - The applicants include Fred Ngatia, Philip Murgor and Alice Jepkoech Yano

    Thirteen Kenyans have made applications to become the next chief justice.

    The applicants are seeking to succeed David Maraga who retired on Tuesday, January 12, upon the attainment of 70 years.

    The chief justice hopefuls' list was unveiled by the Judicial Service Commission on Wednesday, February 10, including nine other people who applied to become the judge of the Supreme Court.

    Surprisingly, none of the Supreme Court judges, including Deputy Chief Justice Philomena Mwilu and Judge Njoki Nding'u applied for the top job at the Judiciary.

    13 applicants

    Below is the list of the applicants among them five judges;

    1. Prof. Otinga Mare

    2. Prof. Moni Wekesa

    3. Justice William Ouko

    4. Justice D.K Marete

    5. Alice Jepkoech Yano

    6. Lady Justice Martha Koome

    7. Justice Mathew Nduma

    8. Fred Ngatia

    9. Prof. Patricia K. Mbote

    10. Philip Kipchirchir Murgor

    11. Said Juma Chitembwe

    12. Otondi Ontweka

    13. Ombongi Brian Matagaro

    As provided in the First Schedule of the Judicial Service Act, the Commission will proceed to shortlisting and publicising of the list within 14 days.

    The JSC will then conduct reference checks to review, verify and supplement information provided by applicants.

    The Commission will also communicate to the applicants’ referees and former employers to get the applicants' background information.

    Supreme Court judge applicants

    Nine applicants who applied to become Supreme Court judge following the retirement of Justice Jackton Ojwang who retired in February 2020, include:

    1. William Ouko

    2. Justice D.K. Marete

    3. Alice Jepkoech Yano

    4. Lady Justice Martha Koome

    5. Justice Joseph Sergon

    6. Justice Mathew Nduma

    7. Justice Kathurima M’Inoti

    8. Dr. Justry Lumumba Nyaberi

    9. Said Chitembwe

    As previously reported, Maraga handed over instruments of power to his deputy Philomena Mwilu.

    The court later blocked Mwilu from serving as CJ and from also occupying the deputy chief justice position pending the determination of a case filed by city lawyer Isaiah Mwongela.

    However, on Monday, February 1, Justice Otieno lifted the order for 11 days, pending an inter-partes hearing at the Meru High Court.
