    Why the list of rich Nigerian politicians is imaginary

    - The list of the richest Nigeriansallegedly released by the FBI circulated in some media - Nigerians immediately react to the news, saying with PDP names missing it might be fake - Legit.ng listed five reasons why the report cannot be true

    17 Jul 2024, 00:00

    - The list of the richest Nigerians allegedly released by the FBI circulated in some media

    - Nigerians immediately react to the news, saying with PDP names missing it might be fake

    - Legit.ng listed five reasons why the report cannot be true

    The list of the richest Nigerian politicians allegedly published of the American Federal Bureau of Investigation, the FBI, makes the rounds in the media.

    The report first appeared on May 5, 2016 on Nigeria Infopedia.

    It says that FBI revealed the details of the top richest politicians’ American bank accounts.

    READ ALSO: 5 Nigerians on Forbes 2016 list of world billionaires

    The names listed in the reports include: ex-president Olusegun Obasanjo, former Lagos state Governor, Bola Tinubu, former vice president Atiku Abubakar, former military head of state Ibrahim Babangida, former INEC chairman Attahiru Jega, Maina, Oransanye, Lamido, Talba, Alamyeisegha and Adamu Muazu.

    However, NAIJ.com investigations show that this picture and subsequent publications which appeared based on it are fake.

    Below are five reasons why this report cannot be true:

    1. If it is real, then where are the logos, dates of release and reference numbers?

    2. Ally financial /Detroit. is written in different font (compare with the rest of the 'document', other lines).

    3. A dot after some names of banks and politicians. In several lines the 'dot' is missing. It makes no sense.  A really inconsistent 'document'. Maybe the FBI now recruits the guys who dropped out of school?!

    4. Huge number of errors: Citi group / new York - two errors, the official name is Citigroup Inc.; New York - two letters should be capital.

    Do you believe the FBI posts document with such huge number of typos?! The photo can be created by every person who has a computer and MS Excel, or similar software.

    5. A publication refers to Amanpour Live show on CNN. The problem is, the video version of this show is nowhere to be found online. The conclusion is obvious, such a video simply does not exist.

    According to the official site of the bureau, the mission of the FBI is to protect the American people and uphold the United States Constitution. It has nothing to do with tracing and investigating Nigerians (or other nationals) who do not pose a threat to USA's national interests. The FBI simply doesn't provide such investigations, there are other American agencies.

    The USA earlier said that it would help President Muhammadu Buhari to trace and recover billions of stolen money.
