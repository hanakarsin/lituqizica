    A poll by TUKO.co.ke has shown most Kenyans believe Deputy President William Ruto can fight corruption better if elected as president.

    The survey was conducted on TUKO.co.ke's social media pages between Tuesday, October 5 and Wednesday, October 6.

    On Twitter, the question was framed as: "In your opinion, which of these leaders in Kenya can truly, diligently and consistently fight corruption? Why?".

    Ruto, former prime minister Raila Odinga, former vice president Musalia Mudavadi and others were on the list.

    The DP topped the list with 35.3%, followed by Raila 27.5% while Mudavadi came a distant third with 5%.

    However, 32.1% of participants said none of the three leaders could fight graft.

    On Facebook, opinions were divided.

    TUKO.co.ke sampled some of the reactions:

    Justus Musili:

    "Corruption is not all about leaders, it's a collective responsibility. We need to start from the bottom up."

    Maxwell Axley:

    "Fighting corruption is not for the leader or president we have independent institutions."

    Purity Mutunga:

    "Raila could have been the man but a small handshake corrupted his mind."

    Jane Rumen:

    "We need new ones, all current politicians are corrupt."

    Raphael Maweu:

    "The Man Miguna Miguna. He will dismantle all cartels."

    Rahhamed Arusey:

    "None, we should give power to the military."'

    Another poll shows politicians have no interest in ending corruption

    In August, an online poll conducted by TUKO.co.ke showed most Kenyans believe politicians in leading political parties cannot end corruption.

    Many said the leaders in parties such as ODM, Jubilee, and UDA have no interest in ending the never-ending graft in Kenya.

    A section of Kenyans opined the government need a total overhaul if the war on corruption is to be won.

    Mt Kenya kingpins

    Similarly, a section of Kenyans picked Gatundu South legislator Moses Kuria, and Murang'a governor Mwangi was Iria as potential Mt Kenya kingpins.

    The majority of respondents argued that the two politicians stand out among their Mt Kenya colleagues.

    Those who vouched for Kuria said the lawmaker is always playing the role of a clown but has a brilliant mind on finance and economics and prefers to keep his cards close to his chest.

    Wa Iria scored highly on his development track record as Murang'a county boss, where he initiated various projects aimed at improving agricultural productivity in the region.
