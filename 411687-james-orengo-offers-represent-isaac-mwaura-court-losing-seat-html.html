    James Orengo Offers to Represent Isaac Mwaura in Court after Losing Seat

    - Tanga Tanga senators claimed there was a court order stopping Speaker Lusaka from declaring Mwaura's seat vacant - The Speaker defended himself saying his office had not been served with any court order regarding Mwaura's woes

    09 Mar 2024, 00:00

    - Tanga Tanga senators claimed there was a court order stopping Speaker Lusaka from declaring Mwaura's seat vacant

    - The Speaker defended himself saying his office had not been served with any court order regarding Mwaura's woes

    - Senator Orengo said the MPs were wasting time discussing a gazette notice urging them to challenge the matter in court

    Senate Speaker Ken Lusaka has defended himself against claims he disregarded a court order stopping him from declaring a vacancy in the seat held by Senator Isaac Mwaura.

    The Speaker came under fire from a section of Tanga Tanga members who questioned his impartiality on a matter they claimed had been moved to the court for further direction.

    Senators Susan Kihika, Aaron Cheruiyot and Kipchumba Murkomen claimed they were aware the Speaker had been served with an injunction court order.

    "The issue of Sen. Mwaura disregards an existing court order provided by the high court. Mr Speaker, I expected you would handle differently a matter that involves removal of a fellow senator," Kihika said.

    To this, Lusaka reminded the members that he was not aware of any existing court order, further pleading with the House to allow him to avail documents that preceded Mwaura's woes.

    “At the time I sat here I had not seen any court order. On Tuesday I will avail all the documents that led to that decision,” he Speaker stated.

    Follow legal process

    Rising a point of order, Siaya senator James Orengo explained to the House that the matter they were ventilating on, was now beyond them since the Speaker cannot reverse a decision to declare a set vacant.

    Orengo disclosed that Mwaura had reached to him earlier in the day to seek his legal opinion which he said he generously offered.

    Further, Orengo asked the members to follow proper legal channels if they genuinely wanted to see their colleague off the hook, adding that he was ready to challenge the decision in court.

    "The speaker cannot reverse a decision he has made and gazetted. It is only the High Court that can and some of us are ready to help if we are asked to represent Mwaura...Otherwise, we will be wasting time discussing that matter here because even the president cannot reverse a gazette notice," the minority leader explained.

    Mwaura was nominated under Article 98 (1) (d) of the constitution by the President Uhuru Kenyatta led outfit in 2017 to represent persons living with disabilities.

    The fall of Mwaura

    The political axe fell on him on Tuesday, May 11, months after being expelled from the ruling Jubilee Party over disciplinary violations.

    Mwaura was among the six nominated senators allied to Deputy President William Ruto who were shown the door by the ruling party.

    Others were Millicent Omanga, Seneta Mary Yiane, Waqo Naomi Jilo, Prengei Victor and Iman Falhada Dekow.

    His plight deepened after the Desma Nungo led-tribunal ruled that the party had adhered to due process in dismissing him.

    However, on Wednesday, May 5, the remaining five senators were saved after Nungo declared their expulsion illegal.
